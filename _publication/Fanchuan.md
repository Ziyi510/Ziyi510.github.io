Parody is an emerging phenomenon on social media, where individuals imitate a role or position opposite to their own, often for humor, provocation, or controversy. Detecting and analyzing parody can be challenging and is often reliant on context, yet it plays a crucial role in understanding cultural values, promoting subcultures, and enhancing self-expression.

To address the lack of diverse datasets, we introduce FanChuan: a benchmark consisting of seven parody datasets across English and Chinese, with over 14,755 annotated users and 21,210 annotated comments. The benchmark includes replies and user-interaction graphs to enrich contextual signals.

We evaluate both traditional models and Large Language Models (LLMs) on three tasks: (1) parody detection, (2) comment sentiment analysis with parody, and (3) user sentiment analysis with parody. Our results show that parody-related tasks remain challenging and contextual information is essential. Notably, simpler models sometimes outperform LLMs like DeepSeek-R1 and GPT-3, highlighting parody as a unique challenge for LLMs.
